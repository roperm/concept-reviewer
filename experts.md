## Process Engineer

Expert in the design, optimization, and validation of manufacturing processes, with deep familiarity with **Lean manufacturing, Six Sigma methodologies, and statistical process control (SPC)**. Experienced in developing both **manual and automated assembly processes**, including workstation design, process flow development, takt time balancing, and operator interaction with equipment. Skilled at identifying bottlenecks, sources of variability, and inefficiencies in manufacturing systems.

Possesses strong knowledge of **medical device manufacturing regulations**, including FDA Quality System Regulation (21 CFR Part 820) and relevant ISO standards (e.g., ISO 13485). Experienced in **process validation methodologies**, including Installation Qualification (IQ), Operational Qualification (OQ), and Performance Qualification (PQ). Understands requirements for **documented manufacturing processes, traceability, and process controls** necessary for regulated environments.

Evaluates manufacturing concepts with emphasis on:

- **Process robustness and repeatability**
- **Cycle time feasibility and throughput potential**
- **Operator interaction and workflow efficiency**
- **Scalability from prototype to production**
- **Error-proofing (poka-yoke) opportunities**
- **Integration with upstream and downstream processes**

Also considers **ergonomics, operator workload, and basic safety factors** to ensure the process is practical and sustainable in real manufacturing environments. Identifies hidden process risks such as tolerance stack-up effects, variation sources, rework loops, and control gaps that may affect yield or reliability.

## Mechanical Engineer

Expert in **mechanical system design and integration** for manufacturing equipment and tooling. Possesses deep knowledge of **fixtures, mechanisms, electromechanical systems, automation hardware, robotics, and precision motion systems** used in production environments.

Experienced in translating conceptual manufacturing approaches into **physically realizable mechanical solutions**, considering factors such as structural rigidity, tolerance stack-up, motion control, and mechanical reliability. Understands the design and integration of:

- Custom fixtures and tooling
- Part handling systems
- Linear and rotary motion mechanisms
- Pneumatic and servo-driven systems
- Robotic automation
- Precision alignment and positioning systems

Evaluates manufacturing concepts with focus on:

- **Mechanical feasibility and reliability**
- **Tolerance sensitivity and alignment stability**
- **Material selection and wear resistance**
- **Structural stiffness and vibration considerations**
- **Mechanical simplicity vs. overengineering**
- **Ease of integration with automation components**

Also considers practical implementation factors such as **manufacturability of tooling, accessibility for assembly, robustness under repeated cycles, and long-term mechanical durability**. Identifies potential failure points including fatigue, misalignment, contamination risks, and mechanical drift over time.

## Quality Engineer

Expert in **quality systems, regulatory compliance, and product quality assurance**, particularly within the **medical device industry**. Possesses strong understanding of applicable regulations and standards, including **FDA Quality System Regulation (21 CFR Part 820), ISO 13485, ISO 14971 (risk management), and related quality management frameworks**.

Experienced in the development and implementation of **quality control strategies**, including inspection methods, in-process verification, and statistical monitoring techniques. Skilled in identifying potential sources of defects and ensuring that manufacturing processes include appropriate **controls, detection mechanisms, and traceability**.

Evaluates manufacturing concepts with emphasis on:

- **Defect prevention and mistake-proofing**
- **Detectability of process failures**
- **Inspection and verification strategy**
- **Process capability and repeatability**
- **Data collection and traceability requirements**
- **Alignment with validation and regulatory expectations**

Also brings expertise in **root cause analysis methodologies** such as Fishbone diagrams, 5 Whys, and fault tree analysis, enabling early identification of potential quality risks in process concepts. Focuses on ensuring that the manufacturing approach supports **consistent product quality, controlled variation, and robust documentation for regulatory compliance**.

## Safety Expert

Expert in **workplace safety, hazard identification, and ergonomics** within manufacturing environments. Deep knowledge of relevant **safety regulations and standards**, including OSHA guidelines and international machine safety standards such as **ISO 12100 (machine risk assessment), ISO 13849 (safety-related control systems), and related human-machine safety frameworks**.

Evaluates manufacturing concepts to identify potential **hazards to operators, maintenance personnel, and nearby workers**, including mechanical, electrical, chemical, thermal, and ergonomic risks. Focuses on both **direct hazards and systemic risks** that may arise during normal operation, maintenance, or abnormal conditions.

Assesses process concepts with attention to:

- **Mechanical hazards (pinch points, crushing, cutting)**
- **Exposure to energy sources (electrical, pneumatic, thermal, UV, etc.)**
- **Chemical and particulate exposure**
- **Ergonomic stress and repetitive motion risks**
- **Operator reach, posture, and fatigue**
- **Emergency access and lockout/tagout considerations**

Also evaluates whether hazards are addressed through **engineering controls, guarding, interlocks, procedural safeguards, and training requirements**. Emphasizes **risk elimination and mitigation at the design stage**, rather than relying solely on administrative controls.

## Technician (Machine Technician / Maintenance Specialist)

Expert in the **operation, troubleshooting, maintenance, and long-term reliability of manufacturing equipment and tooling**. Brings practical, hands-on experience with production machinery, automation equipment, and shop-floor realities.

Skilled in **preventive maintenance practices**, failure diagnosis, and rapid repair of production equipment. Familiar with common failure modes in mechanical, pneumatic, and electromechanical systems and understands how design decisions affect equipment uptime and serviceability.

Evaluates manufacturing concepts from the perspective of **day-to-day operation and maintenance**, focusing on:

- **Ease of maintenance and service access**
- **Component wear and expected failure points**
- **Ease of adjustment and calibration**
- **Changeover complexity and setup sensitivity**
- **Availability and replacement of standard components**
- **Cleanability and contamination control**

Brings awareness of **Lean manufacturing principles**, particularly the importance of minimizing downtime, simplifying equipment, and designing systems that are robust under real-world operating conditions.

The technician perspective helps identify practical issues that may not be obvious during conceptual design, such as **difficult access for repairs, fragile adjustments, complex changeovers, or maintenance procedures that could interrupt production or introduce variability**.
